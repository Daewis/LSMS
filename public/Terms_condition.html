LASUED Internship Program

Internship Terms & Conditions

Below are the official Terms and Conditions governing your internship at the Lagos State University of Education (LASUED). All interns are required to comply fully with these rules throughout the program.

  1. Membership in any secret cult will result in immediate expulsion.
  2. Possession, carrying, or use of weapons or explosives is strictly prohibited.
  3. Unruly conduct, threats, or physical fights are prohibited and attract disciplinary action.
  4. Rape, assault, and threats to life are criminal offences, leading to expulsion and possible police involvement.
  5. Use of hard drugs, alcohol, or other prohibited substances is forbidden on campus.
  6. Suspended students are not allowed on campus unless officially invited.
  7. No religious building (Mosque/Chapel) shall be used as a residence.
  8. University auditoria require written approval before use.
  9. Use of foul, obscene, or offensive language is prohibited.
  10. Religious activities are restricted to designated Chapel or Mosque areas.
  11. Vandalizing or damaging University property will attract suspension or expulsion.
  12. Inappropriate dressing (e.g., mini skirts, sagging trousers, tattoos, slippers) will lead to denial of access to facilities.
  13. Interns must comply with daily working hours: 8:00 AM â€“ 4:00 PM, Monday through Friday.

Prohibited Dressing Styles

  • Transparent or revealing dresses, mini skirts, skimpy clothing.
  • Torn or tattered jeans, obscene inscriptions on clothing.
  • Sagging or excessively baggy trousers.
  • Tight-fitting clothing exposing body contours.
  • Obscene or seductive inscriptions on shirts/tops.
  • Improperly worn ties, unbuttoned shirts, flying collars.
  • Face caps or full coverings of the face in class areas.
  • Piercings, tattoos, or earrings by male students.
  • Oversized jewelry, extreme hairstyles, distracting footwear.
  • Wearing slippers (except in hostels or sports grounds).

Offences & Penalties

Any conduct that breaches the matriculation oath or constitutes a criminal offence under Nigerian law shall attract sanctions such as suspension, rustication, or expulsion. Where criminal misconduct is involved, the matter shall also be referred to law enforcement agencies.
